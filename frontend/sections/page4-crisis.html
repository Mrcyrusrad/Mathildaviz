The Crisis: Housing Pressures

Housing instability is the silent epidemic beneath student life. Many are one missed payment away from relocation or homelessness.

The places meant to feel safe — rentals, share houses, student apartments — often become sources of constant anxiety. Insecure housing isn't just about walls and roofs; it's about peace, privacy, and belonging.

Map – % Income Spent on Rent

The map reveals an uncomfortable truth: in Sydney, Melbourne, and Brisbane, students spend over 50% of income on rent. Financially, this is unsustainable; emotionally, it's devastating.

Each shaded region isn't just data — it's a lived story of compromise, couch-surfing, or staying in overcrowded flats.

"Students spending over half their income on rent report double the rate of distress." — CoreLogic & Orygen, 2023

Marimekko – Shared Housing vs Distress

This visualization combines space and stress. As shared housing percentages rise, distress levels follow.

Sharing spaces brings community — but also chaos. Privacy disappears, and small conflicts amplify existing anxieties.

"Even friendship can't compensate for lost space." — Student housing research

Tree Map – Housing Type Breakdown

The housing tree map shows how fragmented the student experience is: share houses dominate, solo living is rare, and on-campus housing is inaccessible to many.

Each block tells a story of compromise — trading security for affordability.

"A home is more than a place to sleep. It's a state of mind." — Housing wellbeing study
