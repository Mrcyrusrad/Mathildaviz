The Grind: Survival Economy

The final form of adaptation is overwork. When money is tight and time is scarce, students sacrifice rest — not out of ambition, but necessity.

This "survival economy" runs on caffeine and guilt: the pressure to work more to afford less.

Stacked Bar – Time Use Breakdown

The bars show where student life disappears: 35 hours for study, 25 for work, 40 for sleep, and just 10 for leisure.

This is more than a schedule — it's a portrait of imbalance. Leisure and recovery shrink as work expands, leaving no margin for mental repair.

"In the survival economy, rest becomes rebellion."

Run Chart – Energy Over Semester

A semester's energy curve starts high and collapses fast. By week 10, motivation runs dry — replaced by autopilot fatigue.

The visual rhythm mirrors burnout: small drops, small recoveries, and then the crash.

"Burnout doesn't arrive suddenly — it accumulates quietly."

Box Plot – Burnout by Employment Type

Students working full-time show the widest burnout range. Academic performance, mental health, and social connection all decline past the 25-hour mark.

The data reinforces one truth: overwork doesn't build resilience — it dismantles it.

"Overwork masquerades as strength until it's too late."
