The Light: What Helps

After all the strain, recovery begins with connection. Support, structure, and small acts of care help rebuild what stress dismantled.

The light returns not through luck, but through community — a friend who listens, a counsellor who validates, a system that adapts.

Bar Chart – Uptake of Support Programs

These bars show growing awareness: more students are using campus counselling, peer mentoring, and online therapy. Uptake is strongest among those who previously reported the highest distress — proof that help works when it's accessible.

"When support is accessible, distress drops by nearly half." — Beyond Blue, 2024

Network Diagram – Wellbeing Correlations

The network view illustrates the ecosystem of recovery. Sleep, exercise, social support, and financial aid all connect to a single hub: wellbeing.

The stronger the connections, the greater the emotional stability. Healing isn't linear — it's relational.

"Connection, not perfection, sustains wellbeing."

Area Chart – Improved Mood (2020–2025)

Over five years, the percentage of students reporting improved mood nearly doubles — from 35% to 55%. This is the outcome of collective care, institutional reform, and individual courage.

The upward slope isn't just data — it's hope quantified.

"The curve of recovery rises when compassion leads."
